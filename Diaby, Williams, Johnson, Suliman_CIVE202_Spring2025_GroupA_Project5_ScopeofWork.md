## Project Goals:

This project will focus on analyzing household drinking water access trends in Sub-Saharan Africa using the JMP WaSH Database, in partnership with The Water Project, a non-governmental organization committed to improving global drinking water access. The primary objective is to evaluate how access to drinking water services has evolved over time across various countries within the Sub-Saharan region, and to identify existing disparities or gaps that require future intervention. The analysis will be conducted through a detailed review of the JMP Household Drinking Water Data, a reputable global source for monitoring progress toward Sustainable Development Goals related to water and sanitation.

## Project Tasks:

## To achieve the project goals, our group has identified the following key tasks to be completed by our team of professional engineers:

Task 1: Download and organize the JMP WaSH household drinking water data

This NGO hired our team to examine the JMP Household water access dataset to analyze trends in drinking water access. The teams of technicians will collect the MJP WaSH household drinking water data from <https://washdata.org/data> by downloading the data by region and by country in Sub-Saharan Africa. There will be two CSVs to upload into Python. The CSV’s will be cleaned up if there are any empty values. The N/A values can be interpolated or imputed to fill the gaps. The technicians will identify the various columns found in the dataset in order to make a valid analysis. Verify that the columns are categorized as the correct data type such as a string, date, or integer.

Task 2: Create a summary table to compare drinking water access across Sub-Sahara Africa

The technicians will create a summary table to compare the drinking water access across different countries in Sub-Sahara Africa using the organized data in task 1. The summary statistics, mean, max, and min, will be calculated in Python. The data will be grouped by country and year to compare the values over time. From the summary tables, observations can be made to compare water access. The minimum and maximum values will make it easy to find countries with the least and most drinking water access.

Task 3: A time series plot of drinking water access over time in Sub-Sahara Africa

The technicians will create time series plots to compare the drinking water access in countries over time in Sub-Sahara Africa. These plots will be made from the data organized in task 2. The technicians will add axis labels, titles, legends, and appropriate color schemes to enhance readability. These visualizations will highlight trends and variances between countries, making it easier for The Water Project to prioritize future efforts and resources. The plots help viewers identify and describe patterns of improvements or declines in water access across countries over time. The color schemes make it easy to point out the countries with the least water access.

Task 4: Analyze the results of the code

An annotated code document will be created to show how each line of code was used to get these findings. A report will be created to discuss the findings in the project. The technicians will compare the differences of the access to drinking water across countries located in the Sub-Sahara region. The results of each table and plot will be explained. The changes in water access over time will be analyzed. The report will also include a section directly addressing the Water Project’s interest in identifying where significant gaps in access remain and offering insights that could guide future strategic planning. Additionally, the project report will provide a thorough discussion of the observed patterns, focusing on regional disparities, notable improvements, stagnations, or regressions, and hypothesized causes where appropriate.
